District Court

  • Roe v Gulf View [2021] NZDC Auckland CIV-2021-004-000734.

    The first adjudicator found in my favor. I was then informed that that judgment was to be thrown out and a re-hearing was granted on grounds that new evidence was going to be entered in. The second adjudicator found in my favor. The 'evidence' that was entered in was not evidence that an electrician had approved the hard-wiring of an electrical applicance contrary to manufacturers installation instructions.

    I was informed that the second judgment, also, was to be thrown out and a re-re-hearing was granted on grounds that there was new evidence. No new evidence was supplied. The third adjudicator found against me and ordered suppression even though nobody had applied for suppression and I had no opportunity to oppose suppression. I cannot see any reason to grant suppression (e.g., to protect vulnerable people). The purpose of court suppression orders is not to hide or conceal that the courts are refusing to uphold the laws.

    I appealed the judgment of the third adjudicator and the judgement was upheld in the District Court. The effect of this is to set precedent (except suppression again was applied by the court) that landlords do not need electricians to sign off on hard-wired electrics in properties that are rented.

    I suppose it is an act of civil disobedience for me to be making public the two judgments against me that were ordered 'supressed' by the courts of New Zealand. The courts of New Zealand do not seem to understand the purpose of suppression. The courts of New Zealand do not appear willing and / or able to uphold the laws of New Zealand. Would you like to do business, with New Zealand?

    I am, obviously, concerned about the ADHB having chosen to involuntarily detain me in conditions of torture when I presented to the ED with physical complaint that they refused to address because they preferred to unlawfully detain me in the name of psychiatry. They say that I was to be detained under the mental health act with 'voluntary' status. They noted that I wanted to leave but was required to say (was locked in, not allowed to leave without an escort to ensure I returned) and that I was there 'voluntarily' as I was required to be.
