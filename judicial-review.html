Supreme Court (NZSC 106 substantive)

  • Roe v University of Auckland (CA132) SC106/2021 [Case will not be heard on appeal.
  • I have asked what the next step is. If there is further avenue to appeal in New Zealand or if I need to look overseas.
The issue here is that the University of Auckland informed me that that my application to MBChB was unsuccessful on the grounds that my rank order score was not high enough for me to be offered a place. When I queried that it turned out they miscalculated my GPA. Instead of them apologising for their mistake and recalculating my rank order score they informed me that it didn't matter what my rank order score was because I was ineligible to have applied anyway. The Education Act says that domestic adults are eligible to be enrolled in the programme they applied to, however. Therefore the University was wrong to have advised me that I was required to undertake further study in order to become eligible. I would not have enrolled in the MPhil or written that thesis except that the Univesity told me they required me to complete a new programme of study in order to become eligible. Then the University of Waikato did everything they could to prevent and prohibit the timely completion of my Degree so that Auckland could decline my application saying I was ineligible to have applied. They set me up to fail in other words. Which is not just. Which is not fair. I did what they asked of me and they had already made up their minds in advance that they were never going to select me.
