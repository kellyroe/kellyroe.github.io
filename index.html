Forget about Harry Potter. Seriously... Play Hogwarts Legacy, not because it is anything at all to do with Harry Potter. Play it simply because it is AAA gaming at its very very best. It is an incredible game. Best in class in so many ways. Room to improve, of course. But it advances so many aspects.

For example, it is very well engineered. I mean to say the UI is wonderful. I am interested to know whether people playing on computer are using a console controller (which 'system' of controller they prefer (Nintendo, Playstation, X-Box key binding) or whether they are using keyboard and mouse...

It is a difficult game to play in many respects. Mostly because of the complicated or complex key bindings. Or use of the controller. But it is so well engineered. I mean to say, the number of actual things is kept... Manageable. The number of different enemy types, the number of potion types, the number of forigables. Everything is kept manageable. And so that means that everything gets it's proper use. I mean to say, it feels like something that could be mastered, if that makes sense. A lot of old school RPGs were a bit too complicated. So you don't end up using potions or scrolls, say. But a lot of new school console RPGs are a bit too simple... This one, IMHO, feels nice to play. It is simple in so many ways. It isn't trying to be complicated. But it does get complicated all by itself.

I do not know what is going on with the broom control and the camera rotation. Something feels off, but I don't know what it is. The camera doesn't budge and then it swings wildly. There are settings you can play with. So many settings that you can play with...

I really like the way the game is broken down into main quest, exploration, etc. That way you can aim to play a balanced game, if that makes sense. Decide how to spend your time. It is perhaps a bit of a shame it doesn't have the world that levels with you like Elder Scrolls. I mostly feel over-leveled and I haven't done much exploration... But it's common to play on higher difficulties if you are not just mainlining the main quest line, I suppose. I do not claim to be any good. The dualing prompts are nice, too. And the fact that there aren't too many spells. It's a bit of a feat getting used to the key bindings / controller, though, yeah.

I think I have a bit more trouble than most because of playing different games (e.g., different games put jump on different keys. Then also remembering where the keys live on the different controllers (thanks Nintendo). But then you are changing what keys are on your slots so it's about being flexible, I guess. Yeah. Learning to get good with the controller. I remember that was the trouble with second life. They thought the learning curve was too steep on naviating the environment. Let's see how the kids and older people alike find playing a game like Hogwarts.

They do make use of a pointer at various points. So that is a bit slow. Not having a mouse. Shame they aren't using track-pad functionality that is built into the playstation controller for that? I don't know... I would be interested to play it with keyboard and mouse to compare... But that would involve a PC or... A SteamDeck, of course. That allows you to purchase games for portable and docking and use a keyboard and mouse rather than having to purchase a console port of the game which restricts your use of controller... Hrm...

I mean, it is like they are turning (trying to turn) the wizarding world into a RPG. I mean to say, that is basically what they are doing with the game. It is a RPG set in the wizarding world. And they take care to develop the world to nod to other schools in other countries and so on so that there is a sense of the world getting bigger rather than closing in around Hogwarts as the centre of the Universe.

One aspect that they don't do (that is perhaps a bit strange) is... Well, Fire Emblem Three Houses did the idea of you having different relationships with different characters on the basis of what house you picked.
