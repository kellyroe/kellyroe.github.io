The work that I had done in the Philosophy of Psychopathology was all based on the assumption that the aim was to help people rather than abuse people.

So, for example, extending work that had been done on delusions to a form of talk therapy that was being introduced to New Zealand at the time so that therapy may become part of a viable treatment for delusions.

If the aim or the point or the purpose of a diagnosis of delusions (related diagnosis of psychosis) is to keep people impoverished and abused in psychiatric institutions (detain them in conditions of torture, even), then you can see quite a different function for diagnosing and prospects for 'treating' delusions, however.

New Zealand does not appear to have Psychiatry.

`Sane in insane places' was an experiment that was done (or was an idea of an experiment that was done) in psychiatric facilities in the US. The idea was that people presented to the ED saying they heard voices say things like 'thump' and 'thud'. The idea was to see how they would be treated. To record whether they were admitted as in-patients to psychiatric facilities, for how long they were kept in psychiatric facilities. There is controversy over whether the experiment was done, or whether it was done the way it is recorded as having been done, but it is interesting to think about what would happen to people if they were to present similarly to EDs in New Zealand. For how long would they be kept in the psychiatric facilities of New Zealand? Would they be able to get psychiatric assessments, at all, or would that not be possible since people presenting to the ED with suicidality etc are, sometimes, not able to get psychiatric assessments, at all.

I did not intend to try and replicate that experiment. But I did know of it. I am very concerned, indeed, that our hospital system is functioning more as an unlawful detention camp than a hospital.

In 2020 I presented to the ED of the Auckland City Hospital seeking treatment for a physical complaint. Previous injury has resulted in disfigured and numb toes. I had been walking extensively during lock-down and ended up with nails coming off, bacterial infection, and I was concerned about possible hookworm infestation from walking barefoot in the Domain. I went to the ED rather than my GP because, at that time, the media was reporting that GPs did not have PPE and, at that point in time, the EDs were relatively empty.

They mocked my concern about parasites. They placed an IV line in my arm and held me in a seclusion room under 'duty of care' for more than 12 hours. There were extensive cords from medical equipment. There was no natural light. There was an LED that was strobing and medical equipment with regular intemittent beeps. I was not allowed to phone the police or a lawyer. These sorts of sensory deprivation conditions are known to induce delusions in at least some subjects.

They sectioned me for a 5 day observation hold under the Mental Health Act on grounds they believed me to be delusional because I had spoken about parasites. They told me I was required to take medication (threatening to inject me if I did not take it orally) but no prescription had been charted.

After 5 days observation they gave me a form saying I was now relased from involuntary status and was now to be kept in hospital under the Mental Health Act with 'voluntary' status. They refused to allow me to leave for another few days keeping me locked up and they asserted that I was choosing to stay voluntarily in the hospital. I was not. They refused to allow me to phone the police. They refused to supply a duty lawyer.

So, the problem is not that there isn't enough resources for everybody who wants help to have help. The problem is bullying and abuse.
