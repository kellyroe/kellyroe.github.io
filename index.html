The work that I had done in the Philosophy of Psychopathology was all based on the assumption that we were trying to help people in ways they wanted to be helped rather than engaging in a process of harming people.

So, for example, I was trying to extend work that had been done on delusions so as to help people with delusions not be delusional anymore.

But if the aim or the point or the purpose is to unlawfully detain (in conditions of torture, even) as many people as you can... Well, then, you can see a different function, entirely, for the diagnosing and 'treating' of delusions.

New Zealand has been exposed to Psychiatry. For example, Dr Melphi from the Sopranoes. Dr Szasz: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Szasz. New Zealand has the idea of it. Such that they present to GPs or the ED, asking for help from Psychiatry, specifically. They invisage a combination of excluding medical problems, prescribing medication where appropriate, and monitoring the prescriptions in conjunction with regular psychotherapy.

New Zealand does not appear to have Psychiatry.

I presented to the ED of the Auckland City Hospital seeking treatment for a physical complaint. Previous injury has resulted in disfigured and numb toes. I had been walking extensively during lock-down and ended up with nails coming off, bacterial infection, and I was concerned about possible hookworm infestation from walking barefoot in the Domain.

I presented to the ED rather than my GP because, at that time, the media was reporting that GPs did not have PPE and, at that point in time, the EDs were relatively empty.

They mocked my concern about parasites. They placed an IV line in my arm and held me in a seclusion room under 'duty of care' for more than 12 hours. There were extensive cords from medical equipment. There was no natural light. There was an LED that was strobing and medical equipment with regular intemittent beeps. I was not allowed to phone the police or a lawyer. These sorts of sensory deprivation conditions are likely to induce delusions in most people.

They sectioned me for a 5 day observation hold under the Mental Health Act on grounds they believed me to be delusional. They required me to take medication and there is no medication that was charted for me, at all.

After 5 days they gave me a form saying I was to be kept under the Mental Health Act with 'voluntary' status. They refused to allow me to leave and asserted that I was choosing to stay voluntarily in the hospital. I was not.

The problem is not that there isn't enough resources for everybody who wants help to have help. The problem is the bullying and abuse.

They would rather treat me like that than train me to do Medicine. They would be correct that I'm not exactly Dr Leeks 2.0 material. I never did find Dr Leeks motivating or inspiring. I never wanted to be like that or be like him. I never wanted to train under people like that or like him. That is not Psychiatry. This is not a Health System. They don't see the people presenting as people. Cattle or lambs to the slaughter. People survive in spite of our 'health system'. Not because of it.
