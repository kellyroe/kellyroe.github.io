I saw something recently about phrases that our politicians use. Interesting. Really. The speech analysis. Another thing, though, that wasn't mentioned... Most people say a lot of 'um' and 'hmm' and so on. Space fillers, basically. While they are thinking. Noice that they make that means 'I have heard what you have said and I am currently wracking my brain for a sensible answer so what is coming out of my mouth right now is simply designed to block further speech from you because I am working over what you have said already and doing my very very genuine best to come up with a sensible response'. That kind of 'blocking'. Most people say a lot of 'um' and 'well' and 'I see'.. Maybe even rephrase the question... To buy them some thinking time.

There is also something that happens with southern vs northern speakers, in the US, I think studies were done on this... In terms of the rate of speech and the amount of pause. So, the flow of speech was longer in the south. So you had more thinking time before the other interpreted you as having given up your conversational turn. It could be interpreted by non southern speakers as a slowness of thought. But, it was filled with less fillers like 'yes' and 'mmm' and so on.
