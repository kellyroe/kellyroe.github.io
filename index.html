This is a website that I put together relatively quickly so that people can see evidence of some of the things that I have done.

The writing page contains a variety of writing samples where I am the first, or only author. The samples range from fairly tightly written technical pieces in philosophy and psychology to longer explorations or investigations where the role of the philosopher is supposed to be the 'critic and conscience of society'. You will find samples of my undergrad and honors research and coursework. A MA thesis, MPhil thesis, PhD thesis. A co-authored book chapter.

The seminars page contains more than a dozen seminars that were presented to departmental seminars or international conferences. I have provided the written version of the talk and powerpoint presentation for the majority of them.

The teaching page provides a list of courses I have taught and the subject contents that I was sub-contracted to develop to help the students understand the contents of a texbook that I was told to teach from.

The judicial review page provides a list of legal proceedings that I have filed with various courts of New Zealand. Presently, the Social Security Appeals Authority is required to pass judgment on whether or not the Ministry of Social Development's decision to refuse to provide advance benefit payment for security for costs set by court of appeal on grounds that 'Justice is not an essential need' is to be upheld or to be overturned. There was a substantive hearing of this matter in September of 2022 and judgment is awaited.

Neil Quigley instructed me to complain to the Office of the Ombudsman and then take the University to Court if I was unhappy with how the University of Waikato handled my enrolment etc in 2018-2019. I was very unhappy with them (1) refusing to enrol me in the programme I applied to (2) recording I had been enrolled in programmes I never applied to. (3) Refusing to get a thesis that I submitted for examination out to external examiners so as to prevent them accepting it. (4) Sending a thesis I submitted for MPhil examination out for 'doctoral reports' to be written (sending it out to be examined for the wrong degree). (5) Refusing to base the outcome of external examination on the reports of external examiners. How could any of this be considered acceptable?
